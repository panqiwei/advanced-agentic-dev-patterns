<Band tone="tan">
  <CoreDef>Methods that leverage general computation (search and learning) eventually win by a large margin. Building human domain knowledge into systems works short-term but stalls or blocks long-term progress.</CoreDef>
</Band>

<Band tone="paper-raised" grow>
  <Quote cite="Sutton 2019">We want meta-methods that can find and capture complexity, not ones that build in what we think we already found.</Quote>
  <div class="bl-pattern">
    <div class="pattern-step p1">
      <span class="ps-n">1</span>
      <span class="ps-text">Researcher sees a model limit → adds compensating machinery to the harness</span>
    </div>
    <div class="pattern-arrow">↓ model upgrades</div>
    <div class="pattern-step p2">
      <span class="ps-n">2</span>
      <span class="ps-text">Compensation becomes dead weight → "context anxiety" reset evaporates on the new model</span>
    </div>
    <div class="pattern-arrow">↓ right direction</div>
    <div class="pattern-step p3">
      <span class="ps-n">3</span>
      <span class="ps-text">"What can I stop doing?" — delete stale assumptions, hand sovereignty back to the model</span>
    </div>
  </div>
</Band>

<Band tone="paper-sunk" dense>
  <p class="os-analogy">OS analogy: the <code>read()</code> syscall has fit every storage medium from the 1970s to today — because it bakes in no specific hardware knowledge. The durability of meta-interfaces vindicates the Bitter Lesson.</p>
</Band>
