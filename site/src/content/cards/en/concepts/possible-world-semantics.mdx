<Band tone="paper-raised" dense>
  <h2 class="card-title">Possible World Semantics</h2>
  <p class="card-subtitle">Possible World Semantics — in the closest world to the actual where A holds, C also holds</p>
</Band>

<Band tone="tan">
  <CoreDef>A counterfactual "if A, then C" is true iff some A∧C world is closer to the actual world than any A∧¬C world. Closeness = comparative similarity, a multi-dimensional trade-off between matching natural laws and matching particular facts. Lewis 1973.</CoreDef>
</Band>

<Band tone="paper-raised" grow>
  <div class="similarity-rules">
    <Kicker>Similarity-weighting rules (Lewis)</Kicker>
    <div class="sr-row r1"><span class="sr-n">high weight</span><span class="sr-desc">preserve natural laws: fewer miracles (violations of law) = closer</span></div>
    <div class="sr-row r2"><span class="sr-n">mid weight</span><span class="sr-desc">large-scale fact match: share with the actual world a larger region of perfectly matching spacetime</span></div>
    <div class="sr-row r3"><span class="sr-n">trade-off</span><span class="sr-desc">one small localized miracle can buy a large region of matching facts; many big miracles cannot</span></div>
  </div>
  <div class="comparison-box">
    <Kicker>Possible-world semantics vs interventionist semantics</Kicker>
    <div class="cb-row cb-header"><span>Dimension</span><span>Possible worlds (Lewis)</span><span>Intervention (Pearl)</span></div>
    <div class="cb-row"><span>How to evaluate</span><span>find the closest possible world</span><span>surgical intervention on a causal model</span></div>
    <div class="cb-row"><span>What is fixed</span><span>the most similar fact-configuration</span><span>variables causally independent of the antecedent</span></div>
