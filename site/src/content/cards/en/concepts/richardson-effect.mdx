<Band tone="paper-raised" dense>
  <h2 class="card-title">Richardson Effect</h2>
  <p class="card-subtitle">Richardson Effect — the shorter the measuring unit, the longer an irregular boundary measures</p>
</Band>

<Band tone="tan">
  <CoreDef>Richardson (circa 1951) found that Portugal and Spain reported their shared border differing by 23% (987 km vs 1214 km). Root cause: the two countries used different-length "rulers." More surprising: certain boundaries grow toward infinity as the unit approaches zero — the exact opposite of Euclidean expectation.</CoreDef>
</Band>

<Band tone="paper-raised" grow>
  <div class="visual-intuition">
    <Kicker>Intuition: walking a coastline</Kicker>
    <div class="vi-row vi100"><span class="vi-scale">100 km stride</span><span class="vi-desc">Cuts across many small bays and capes — shorter path</span></div>
    <div class="vi-row vi1"><span class="vi-scale">1 km stride</span><span class="vi-desc">Walks into each bay — longer path</span></div>
    <div class="vi-row vi1m"><span class="vi-scale">1 m stride</span><span class="vi-desc">Rounds every rock — longer still</span></div>
    <div class="vi-note">Each shorter stride isn't "more accurate" — it measures a genuinely longer path</div>
  </div>
  <div class="math-connection">
    <Kicker>Mandelbrot's formalization</Kicker>
    <KV label="Formula">{"L(ε) ~ F·ε^(1-D) — where D is the fractal dimension (non-integer Hausdorff dimension)"}</KV>
    <KV label="What D means">The more D exceeds 1, the more jagged the boundary and the faster the divergence; D=1 means a rectifiable curve</KV>
    <KV label="Non-rectifiability">The Richardson effect is the measurement-level fingerprint of a non-rectifiable curve</KV>
  </div>
</Band>
