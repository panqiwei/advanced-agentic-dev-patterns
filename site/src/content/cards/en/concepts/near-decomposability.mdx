<Band tone="paper-raised" dense>
  <h2 class="card-title">Near-Decomposability</h2>
  <p class="card-subtitle">Near-Decomposability — subsystems are approximately independent short-term, coupled long-term through aggregates</p>
</Band>

<Band tone="tan">
  <CoreDef>{"Simon's (1962) core insight: complex systems can be approximately decomposed into hierarchical sub-modules — interactions inside a sub-module are frequent (high frequency), interactions between sub-modules are sparse (low frequency). Frequency separation lets analysis and design proceed layer-by-layer rather than handling all couplings at once."}</CoreDef>
</Band>

<Band tone="paper-raised" grow>
  <div class="nd-split">
    <div class="nd-side nd-short">
      <span class="nd-label">Short-run behavior</span>
      <span class="nd-body">Subsystems approximately independent — each can be analyzed alone without distortion</span>
      <span class="nd-example">Intracellular metabolism vs. intercellular signaling</span>
    </div>
    <div class="nd-side nd-long">
      <span class="nd-label">Long-run behavior</span>
      <span class="nd-body">Subsystem aggregates couple — overall behavior set by slow cross-module variables</span>
      <span class="nd-example">Organ systems vs. single-cell mechanisms</span>
    </div>
  </div>
  <div class="pace-layers">
    <Kicker>Frequency separation / pace layers</Kicker>
    <div class="pl-row"><span class="pl-freq">fast →</span><span class="pl-desc">Within-component mechanism: high-frequency interaction, local optimization</span></div>
    <div class="pl-row"><span class="pl-freq">mid →</span><span class="pl-desc">Module interface: periodic sync, passing aggregate state</span></div>
    <div class="pl-row"><span class="pl-freq">slow →</span><span class="pl-desc">System constraints: low-frequency evolution, shaping overall structure</span></div>
  </div>
  <KV label="Agent application">Tool execution (fast) vs. session state (mid) vs. harness interface (slow) — three layers, each designed and optimized separately</KV>
</Band>
