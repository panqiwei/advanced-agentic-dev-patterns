<Band tone="paper-raised" dense>
  <h2 class="card-title">Difference-Making</h2>
  <p class="card-subtitle">Difference-Making — the causal core across theoretical boundaries</p>
</Band>

<Band tone="tan">
  <CoreDef>If a factor's presence or absence changes whether the effect occurs, that factor is a difference-maker for the effect. The idea cuts across regularity, counterfactual, and interventionist theories — it's the common core of multiple causal theories.</CoreDef>
</Band>

<Band tone="paper-raised" grow>
  <PillarGrid cols={2}>
    <Pillar label="Regularity theory">The non-redundancy requirement of INUS conditions — remove the factor and its sufficient cluster no longer suffices — is difference-making logicized</Pillar>
    <Pillar label="Counterfactual theory">The most direct expression: had C not occurred, E would not have. Lewis 2000 "influence" version: variation in C drives corresponding variation in E</Pillar>
    <Pillar label="Inferentialist theory">Strevens' kairetic account: every member of an explanatory core must make a difference to the effect; three kinds of non-difference-makers are excluded</Pillar>
    <Pillar label="Interventionism">Woodward: C makes a difference to E ⟺ there exists some intervention on C that changes the value of E</Pillar>
  </PillarGrid>
</Band>

<Band tone="paper-sunk" dense>
  <p class="strevens-note">Strevens' refinement: finer-grained than simple counterfactual dependence — distinguishes (1) factors that don't participate in the process at all; (2) participants whose effect is negligible; (3) descriptions that are too precise ("a four-kilogram brick" vs "a rigid object above the threshold").</p>
</Band>
