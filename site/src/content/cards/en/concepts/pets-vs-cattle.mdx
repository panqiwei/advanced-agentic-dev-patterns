<Band tone="paper-raised" dense>
  <h2 class="card-title">Pets vs Cattle</h2>
  <p class="card-subtitle">Pets vs Cattle — disposability is the point: if any component can be killed and replaced, it's cattle</p>
</Band>

<Band tone="tan">
  <CoreDef>Pets: unique individuals you can't afford to lose; failures demand special handling. Cattle: interchangeable herd members; a failure just means replacing one. Randy Bias (2011) lifts this from infrastructure to agent architecture: component disposability = failures that don't propagate into system failures.</CoreDef>
</Band>

<Band tone="paper-raised" grow>
  <div class="compare-arch">
    <Kicker>Agent components: pets vs cattle</Kicker>
    <div class="ca-row ca-header"><span>Scenario</span><span>Pet mode (single container)</span><span>Cattle mode (decoupled)</span></div>
    <div class="ca-row"><span>Sandbox failure</span><span class="ca-bad">session lost, no recovery</span><span class="ca-good">provision a new container, session continues</span></div>
    <div class="ca-row"><span>Harness failure</span><span class="ca-bad">debugging requires entering user-data container</span><span class="ca-good">wake(sessionId) recovers from outside</span></div>
    <div class="ca-row"><span>Fault isolation</span><span class="ca-bad">all failures look alike</span><span class="ca-good">pinpoint the failing layer</span></div>
    <div class="ca-row"><span>Privacy</span><span class="ca-bad">pet container holds user data</span><span class="ca-good">credential isolation; replace only the broken component</span></div>
  </div>
  <div class="enabling-conditions">
    <Kicker>Preconditions</Kicker>
    <KV label="Brain-hand decoupling">Meta-Harness separating cognition (Session) from execution (Sandbox) is the prerequisite for cattle-ification</KV>
    <KV label="Externalized session">session must persist independently, not live and die with harness/sandbox</KV>
  </div>
</Band>
