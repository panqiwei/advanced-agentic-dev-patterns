  <p class="card-subtitle">Teleological Explanation — center on a thing's end (telos); regularities demand explanation, coincidence is not enough</p>
</Band>

<Band tone="tan">
  <CoreDef>Aristotle's core argument: the arrangement of teeth (sharp incisors, broad molars) is regularly conducive to animal survival. If explained by material causes alone, the beneficial correspondence would be coincidence — but a regular coincidence demands explanation. The final cause is introduced not as a proof but as the best explanation of the regularity. Key: teleological explanation does not depend on mental states — casting a bronze statue does not require the craftsman's desire, only the craft of bronze-casting itself.</CoreDef>
</Band>
