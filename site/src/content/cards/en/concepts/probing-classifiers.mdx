<Band tone="paper-raised" dense>
  <h2 class="card-title">Probing Classifiers</h2>
  <p class="card-subtitle">Probing Classifiers — freeze the model, train a simple classifier on activations to check if specific information is encoded</p>
</Band>

<Band tone="tan">
  <CoreDef>Freeze pretrained model → extract layer activations → train a simple probe (linear/MLP) → evaluate on a test set. High accuracy means the layer encodes the target attribute. The core methodological caveat: high probe accuracy ≠ the model "uses" that information.</CoreDef>
</Band>

<Band tone="paper-raised" grow>
  <div class="probe-types">
    <Kicker>Three probe types</Kicker>
    <div class="pt-row linear"><span class="pt-name">Linear probe</span><span class="pt-use">Tests the linear representation hypothesis; efficient, interpretable</span><span class="pt-limit">Misses non-linear encodings</span></div>
    <div class="pt-row mlp"><span class="pt-name">Non-linear probe (MLP)</span><span class="pt-use">Higher capacity ceiling, serves as control</span><span class="pt-limit">High accuracy may reflect the probe's own modeling power</span></div>
    <div class="pt-row sparse"><span class="pt-name">Sparse probe</span><span class="pt-use">Pinpoints single neurons, strong interpretability</span><span class="pt-limit">Limited coverage</span></div>
  </div>
  <div class="validation-ladder">
    <Kicker>Validation ladder: from correlation to causation</Kicker>
    <KV label="Generalization tests">Block-held-out tests: if the probe still generalizes after excluding a data slice, the model learned geometric structure rather than memorization</KV>
    <KV label="Causal intervention">Ablation or targeted activation intervention — verifies a neuron's causal effect on model output, not just correlation</KV>
    <KV label="Othello lesson">Label frame (absolute color vs relative color) can flip a linear probe's verdict — probes are highly sensitive to the labeling scheme</KV>
  </div>
</Band>
