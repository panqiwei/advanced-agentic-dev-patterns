<Band tone="paper-raised" dense>
  <h2 class="card-title">Systematicity</h2>
  <p class="card-subtitle">Systematicity — cognitive abilities are structurally linked: whoever understands P must understand P's structural neighbors</p>
</Band>

<Band tone="tan">
  <CoreDef>Someone who understands "John loves Mary" must also understand "Mary loves John" — not because of task difficulty, but because possessing the relevant concepts constitutively grants that ability. Fodor & Pylyshyn (1988): "Any architecture that makes systematicity accidental is the wrong architecture." This throws the ball to connectionism: if a neural net's handling of structural variants is a training accident, that architecture cannot explain human cognition.</CoreDef>
</Band>
