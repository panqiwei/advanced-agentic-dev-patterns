<Band tone="paper-raised" dense>
  <h2 class="card-title">Probability of Causation</h2>
  <p class="card-subtitle">Probability of Causation — the probability that a given factor "was" the cause of an event that already happened</p>
</Band>

<Band tone="tan">
  <CoreDef>Probability of causation answers "causes of effects" questions: did this drug cause my illness? Three forms: PN (probability of necessity, matching the legal but-for test), PS (probability of sufficiency), PNS (necessary and sufficient). Pearl proves these are identifiable from data under monotonicity.</CoreDef>
</Band>

<Band tone="paper-raised" grow>
  <div class="three-pocs">
    <Kicker>Three probabilities of causation</Kicker>
    <div class="poc-row pn">
      <span class="poc-code">PN</span>
      <span class="poc-name">Necessity</span>
      <span class="poc-def">{"P(Y_{x'} = y' | X=x, Y=y) — without X, would Y not be y? The legal but-for test"}</span>
    </div>
    <div class="poc-row ps">
      <span class="poc-code">PS</span>
      <span class="poc-name">Sufficiency</span>
      <span class="poc-def">{"P(Y_x = y | X=x', Y=y') — with X, would Y be y?"}</span>
    </div>
    <div class="poc-row pns">
      <span class="poc-code">PNS</span>
      <span class="poc-name">Both</span>
      <span class="poc-def">Same individual: X produces Y, and absence of X prevents Y — the strongest form of causation</span>
    </div>
  </div>
  <div class="identification">
    <Kicker>Identification</Kicker>
    <KV label="Monotonicity">When Y_1(u) ≥ Y_0(u) holds for every individual, PN is fully identified</KV>
    <KV label="Surprising result">Combining experimental + observational data, PN lower bound can reach 1 even when neither source alone shows causation</KV>
    <KV label="Rung 3 on the ladder">PN requires counterfactuals — observation or intervention alone cannot answer</KV>
  </div>
</Band>
