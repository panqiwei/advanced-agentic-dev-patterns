<Band tone="paper-raised" dense>
  <h2 class="card-title">Propositional Fixation</h2>
  <p class="card-subtitle">Propositional Fixation — neural networks cannot represent full first-order or higher-order logic</p>
</Band>

<Band tone="tan">
  <CoreDef>McCarthy's diagnosis: neural nets' fundamental limit is not an implementation detail — it follows from their computational structure. Distributed continuous representations cannot realize arbitrarily deep recursive binding and quantification. This is the core theoretical motivation for neurosymbolic integration.</CoreDef>
</Band>

<Band tone="paper-raised" grow>
  <div class="logic-boundary">
    <Kicker>Logical reach of neural nets</Kicker>
    <div class="lb-row can">
      <span class="lb-status can-label">Can</span>
      <span class="lb-content">Propositional logic · non-monotonic logic programs · propositional modal logic · finite fragments of first-order logic</span>
    </div>
    <div class="lb-row cannot">
      <span class="lb-status cant-label">Cannot</span>
      <span class="lb-content">Full first-order logic (with function symbols, unbounded quantification chains) · higher-order logic</span>
    </div>
  </div>
  <div class="engineering-impacts">
    <Kicker>Engineering implications</Kicker>
    <KV label="Relational ceiling">Learning "ancestor" (unbounded recursion) requires a symbolic reasoning layer — pure neural cannot extrapolate</KV>
    <KV label="Generalization bound">Learned rules are propositional-level (grounded); they do not compose and extrapolate to new variables</KV>
    <KV label="Response 1">LTN / Logic Tensor Networks: turn logic statements into loss constraints — approximate, not exact</KV>
    <KV label="Response 2">Neurosymbolic hybrids: leave exact inference to the symbolic layer (Type 3–6), let the neural layer handle perception/learning</KV>
  </div>
</Band>
