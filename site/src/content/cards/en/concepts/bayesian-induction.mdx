<Band tone="tan">
  <CoreDef>Assign a prior to a hypothesis, update to a posterior by Bayes' rule after observation, then predict. A precise observation-to-prediction path — but the choice of prior introduces an irreducible assumption, which is where empirical commitments enter the inference.</CoreDef>
</Band>

<Band tone="paper-raised" grow>
  <Formula>p(H | E) = p(E | H) · p(H) / p(E)</Formula>

    </div>
    <div class="bc-row">

<Band tone="paper-sunk" dense>
  <div class="three-problems">
    <KV label="status of priors">principle of indifference leads to Bertrand's paradox; subjectivism accepts priors as personal belief — there is no "unbiased" model</KV>
    <KV label="No Free Lunch">under a uniform distribution every algorithm has expected generalization error = 1/2; effective learning depends on model guarantees relative to specific assumptions</KV>
    <KV label="rule of succession">100 white balls → next-white probability = 101/102 ≈ 0.99, not 1 — inductive conclusions stay probabilistic</KV>
  </div>
</Band>
