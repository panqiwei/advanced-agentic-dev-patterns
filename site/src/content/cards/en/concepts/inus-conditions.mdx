<Band tone="paper-raised" dense>
  <h2 class="card-title">INUS Conditions</h2>
  <p class="card-subtitle">Insufficient but Non-redundant part of an Unnecessary but Sufficient condition</p>
</Band>

<Band tone="tan">
  <CoreDef>What we ordinarily call "the cause" is usually neither sufficient nor necessary for the effect but an indispensable factor within some sufficient cluster. The account admits parallel causal paths (disjunction); each path is a minimally sufficient conjunctive cluster.</CoreDef>
</Band>

<Band tone="paper-raised" grow>
  <div class="formula-band">
    <Kicker>Disjunction-of-conjunctions structure</Kicker>
    <div class="formula">
      <span>(C₁₁ ∧ C₁₂ ∧ …) ∨ (C₂₁ ∧ C₂₂ ∧ …) ∨ … ↔ E</span>
    </div>
    <p class="formula-note">Each conjunct = a minimally sufficient cluster; each factor = an INUS condition (cannot be removed from its cluster)</p>
  </div>
  <div class="inus-decode">
    <div class="inus-row"><span class="inus-letter">I</span><span class="inus-full">Insufficient</span><span class="inus-desc">Not enough on its own to produce E</span></div>
    <div class="inus-row"><span class="inus-letter">N</span><span class="inus-full">Non-redundant</span><span class="inus-desc">Cannot be removed from its cluster (the regularity-world analog of difference-making)</span></div>
    <div class="inus-row"><span class="inus-letter">U</span><span class="inus-full">Unnecessary</span><span class="inus-desc">Its cluster is not necessary for E (other clusters can also produce E)</span></div>
    <div class="inus-row"><span class="inus-letter">S</span><span class="inus-full">Sufficient</span><span class="inus-desc">Its cluster as a whole is sufficient for E</span></div>
  </div>
  <div class="problems">
    <KV label="Residual problem: spurious causation">Siren → workers in London clock off; common cause is 5 p.m. — INUS cannot rule out joint effects of common causes</KV>
    <KV label="NESS successor (law)">Necessary Element of a Sufficient Set — now a core tool in legal causation theory</KV>
  </div>
</Band>
