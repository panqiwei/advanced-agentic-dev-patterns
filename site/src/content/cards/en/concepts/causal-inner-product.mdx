<Band tone="paper-raised" dense>
  <h2 class="card-title">Causal Inner Product</h2>
  <p class="card-subtitle">Causal Inner Product — the "right" geometry for LLM representation space</p>
</Band>

<Band tone="tan">
  <CoreDef>Problem with the Euclidean inner product: the training objective only fixes the distribution, and the same distribution admits infinitely many representations differing by an invertible linear map — so cosine similarity reflects training randomness, not semantics. The causal inner product fixes this.</CoreDef>
</Band>

<Band tone="paper-raised" grow>
  <div class="def-section">
    <Kicker>Core definition</Kicker>
    <p class="def-text">Under the causal inner product, the vectors of causally separable concepts W and Z (independently variable, e.g., "language" and "gender") are orthogonal:</p>
    <Formula>⟨γ̄_W, γ̄_Z⟩_C = 0</Formula>
  </div>
  <div class="construction-section">
    <Kicker>Explicit construction (from the unembedding matrix)</Kicker>
    <Formula>⟨γ̄, γ̄'⟩_C := γ̄ᵀ Cov(γ)⁻¹ γ̄'</Formula>
    <p class="const-note">Cov(γ)⁻¹ is the inverse covariance of the vocabulary unembedding matrix — no extra training or manual labels needed</p>
  </div>
  <div class="unification-section">
    <Kicker>Unifying the three LRH representations</Kicker>
    <p class="unif-text">Under the causal inner product, unembedding and embedding representations are unified by Riesz isomorphism — the same direction vector serves both probes and steering vectors</p>
  </div>
</Band>

<Band tone="paper-sunk" dense>
  <p class="experiment-note">LLaMA-2 7B validation: 27 concepts produce a block-diagonal heatmap under the causal inner product — semantically similar concepts cluster, and causally separable concepts are near-orthogonal.</p>
</Band>
