<Band tone="paper-raised" dense>
  <h2 class="card-title">Structural Causal Model</h2>
  <p class="card-subtitle">Structural Causal Model — a system of structural equations that unifies association, intervention, and counterfactual inference</p>
</Band>

<Band tone="tan">
  <CoreDef>SCM is the meta-framework of causal inference, defined by a triple ⟨U, V, E⟩: U is the set of exogenous (background) variables, V the endogenous variables, E the set of structural equations (one fi per Vi fixing its value). Key point: structural equations describe "generative mechanisms," not statistical regressions — they stay invariant under intervention (modularity), whereas regression coefficients shift when you intervene.</CoreDef>
</Band>

<Band tone="paper-raised" grow>
  <div class="three-rungs">
    <Kicker>The three rungs unified in SCM</Kicker>
    <div class="tr-row tr1">
      <span class="tr-rung">L1 Association</span>
      <span class="tr-query">P(Y=y | X=x)</span>
      <span class="tr-tool">Observational data + graph reading</span>
    </div>
    <div class="tr-row tr2">
      <span class="tr-rung">L2 Intervention</span>
      <span class="tr-query">P(Y=y | do(X=x))</span>
      <span class="tr-tool">Equation truncation + do-calculus</span>
    </div>
    <div class="tr-row tr3">
      <span class="tr-rung">L3 Counterfactual</span>
      <span class="tr-query">P(Yx=y | X=x', Y=y')</span>
      <span class="tr-tool">Twin networks + fixed exogenous variables</span>
    </div>
  </div>
  <div class="distinctions">
    <Kicker>Key distinctions</Kicker>
    <KV label="Structural eq ≠ regression">fi(xi, ui) describes a mechanism; intervention severs only the matching equation. Regression coefficients are global statistics and break under intervention</KV>
    <KV label="DAG is the qualitative skeleton">SCM uses a DAG for qualitative structure and equations for quantitative mechanism — the two complement each other</KV>
    <KV label="Pearl 2010 meta-framework">Unifies six independent causal traditions: RCT, path analysis, graphical models, counterfactuals, decision theory, epidemiology</KV>
  </div>
</Band>
