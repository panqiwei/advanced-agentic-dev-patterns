  <p class="card-subtitle">Constant Conjunction — all that is actually observable in causal inference</p>

<Band tone="tan">
  <CoreDef>When events of type A always precede events of type B in experience, that is constant conjunction. Hume's core insight: this is everything we actually observe in causation. Any "necessary connection" beyond it comes from the mind, not from things.</CoreDef>
</Band>

<Band tone="paper-raised" grow>
  <div class="hume-chain">
    <div class="chain-step cs-obs">
      <span class="cs-label">experiential input</span>
      <span class="cs-content">repeated observation of the A→B constant conjunction</span>
    </div>
    <div class="chain-arrow">↓ habit operates</div>
    <div class="chain-step cs-habit">
      <span class="cs-label">psychological mechanism</span>
      <span class="cs-content">a disposition forms: on seeing A, automatically expect B; the millionth repetition is empirically indistinguishable from the first</span>
    </div>
    <div class="chain-arrow">↓ produces</div>
    <div class="chain-step cs-belief">
      <span class="cs-label">cognitive output</span>
      <span class="cs-content">the idea of necessary connection (the "determination of the mind") + causal belief</span>
    </div>
  </div>
</Band>

<Band tone="paper-sunk" dense>
  <div class="key-distinctions">
    <KV label="conjunction vs connection">constant conjunction = observable fact; necessary connection = mind's projection, not observable</KV>
    <KV label="copy principle in action">simple impressions and simple ideas are themselves in constant conjunction — Hume uses this to infer their causal relation</KV>
    <KV label="modern mapping">scaling laws are a kind of constant conjunction — inferring future trends from regularities in past data</KV>
  </div>
</Band>
