<Band tone="tan">
  <CoreDef>d-separation: Z blocks all paths X→Y ⇒ X ⊥ Y | Z. Conditioning on the middle node blocks chains/forks but opens colliders — the most counter-intuitive source of bias.</CoreDef>
</Band>

<Band tone="paper-sunk" dense>
  <Formula>P(Y | do(X)) = Σ<sub>z</sub> P(Y | X, Z=z) · P(Z=z)</Formula>
  <p class="formula-note">Backdoor adjustment: Z covers every backdoor path and contains no descendant of X</p>
  <p class="pearl-insight">Pearl 2010: <em>missing</em> arrows in the graph matter more than the ones present — a missing edge asserts zero effect, which is the core encoding of a structural assumption.</p>
</Band>
