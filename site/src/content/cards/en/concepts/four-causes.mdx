<Band tone="tan">
  <CoreDef>The four causes aren't competing alternative explanations — they are four explanatory dimensions of the same thing. A complete scientific explanation needs (at most) all four working together. Formal and final causes often coincide; efficient cause is "formally identical" with the formal / final cause.</CoreDef>
</Band>

<Band tone="paper-raised" grow>
  <PillarGrid cols={2}>
    <Pillar label="Material">what is it made of? The substrate of change (hupokeimenon). In natural philosophy, recast as hypothetical necessity — the material conditions required to achieve an end</Pillar>
    <Pillar label="Formal">what is it? The essence (to ti ên einai), definition, internal structural principle. Not outward shape — what makes a thing what it is</Pillar>
    <Pillar label="Efficient">where does the change come from? Most accurately, the bronze-casting art itself, not the craftsman — the knowledge, not its holder. Aims at a non-psychological explanatory mode</Pillar>
    <Pillar label="Final">what is its good? Only "the best one" qualifies. In organisms: the thing without which the organism can't persist + with which it flourishes</Pillar>
  </PillarGrid>
  <div class="pearl-note">
    <span class="pn-label">the boundary of Pearl's SCM</span>
    <p>SCM structural equations precisely formalize the efficient cause — "where change comes from." Final and formal causes have no direct counterpart in modern causal models. That is both the focus of Pearl's framework and its limit.</p>
  </div>
</Band>
