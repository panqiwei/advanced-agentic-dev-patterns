<Band tone="paper-raised" dense>
  <h2 class="card-title">Language of Thought</h2>
  <p class="card-subtitle">Language of Thought Hypothesis — thinking is computation over Mentalese</p>
</Band>

<Band tone="tan">
  <CoreDef>Thinking is computation over syntactically structured symbolic expressions. Mental representations have constituent structure, like sentences; mental processes operate directly on syntactic form (not semantic content); the meaning of complex representations is fixed by compositionality.</CoreDef>
</Band>

<Band tone="paper-raised" grow>
  <div class="three-claims">
    <Kicker>Three core commitments</Kicker>
    <KV label="Representations have syntactic structure">Concepts = Mentalese words; propositions = Mentalese sentences</KV>
    <KV label="Processes are syntax-sensitive">Computation acts on form, not meaning — the Formality Condition</KV>
    <KV label="Compositional semantics">Meaning of a complex representation = systematic function of parts + mode of combination</KV>
  </div>
  <div class="systematicity-arg">
    <Kicker>Strongest argument: systematicity</Kicker>
    <div class="sys-box">
      <span class="sys-if">Able to think "John loves Mary"</span>
      <span class="sys-arrow">→ necessarily able to think</span>
      <span class="sys-then">"Mary loves John"</span>
    </div>
    <p class="sys-note">Under LOTH this is necessary — both thoughts share the same Mentalese parts, only the arrangement changes. Not contingent, architecturally guaranteed.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="llm-question">
    <KV label="LLMs and LOTH">Are high-dimensional activations "continuous Mentalese"? The linear representation hypothesis shows LLMs harbor linearly separable structured representations</KV>
    <KV label="Fodor & Pylyshyn rebuttal">Connectionism acquires compositionality only contingently through training, failing to explain the necessity of systematicity — architectural guarantee vs training contingency</KV>
  </div>
</Band>
