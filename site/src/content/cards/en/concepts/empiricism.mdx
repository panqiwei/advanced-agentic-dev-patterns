<Band tone="tan">
  <CoreDef>All knowledge ultimately derives from experience and observation. Hume extended Newton's experimental method to the "moral sciences" — rejecting any metaphysics that claims to uncover "ultimate principles": claims that transcend all possible experience are not merely false, they are unintelligible.</CoreDef>
</Band>

<Band tone="paper-raised" grow>
  <div class="empiricism-tools">
    <div class="et-item">
      <span class="et-label">Copy Principle</span>
      <p>every simple idea is derived from a corresponding simple impression. The raw material of thought ultimately comes from sensory experience — it can only be "compounded, transposed, augmented, or diminished."</p>
    </div>
    <div class="et-item">
      <span class="et-label">Method of Definition ("the new microscope")</span>
      <p>trace a term back to its idea, then back to its original impression. If any step fails, the idea lacks cognitive content — Hume's principal weapon against traditional metaphysics and theology.</p>
    </div>
  </div>
</Band>

<Band tone="paper-sunk" dense>
  <div class="two-phases">
    <KV label="critical phase">exposes traditional metaphysics that violates empiricist principles — "ultimate principles" beyond experience are unintelligible</KV>
    <KV label="constructive phase">builds a science of human nature on experiential foundations; accepts "I feign no hypotheses"; aims not at certainty but at the best explanation</KV>
  </div>
</Band>
