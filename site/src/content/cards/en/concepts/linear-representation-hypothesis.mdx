<Band tone="paper-raised" dense>
  <h2 class="card-title">Linear Representation Hypothesis</h2>
  <p class="card-subtitle">LRH — for feature f there is a direction w such that f(x) ≈ a(x)·w</p>
</Band>

<Band tone="tan">
  <CoreDef>Features inside neural networks encode linearly in activation space — the presence or strength of a feature can be read by projecting the activation onto its direction vector. The network is globally nonlinear, but specific features occupy locally linearly separable directions.</CoreDef>
</Band>

<Band tone="paper-raised" grow>
  <div class="three-lrh">
    <Kicker>Three equivalent intuitions (Park et al. 2023 unified formalization)</Kicker>
    <div class="lrh-row">
      <span class="lrh-name">Unembedding representation</span>
      <span class="lrh-desc">"king − man + woman = queen" — word-pair differences are collinear</span>
    </div>
    <div class="lrh-row highlight">
      <span class="lrh-name">Measurement representation</span>
      <span class="lrh-desc">Linear probes can predict concepts (logit-linear predictability)</span>
    </div>
    <div class="lrh-row">
      <span class="lrh-name">Embedding representation</span>
      <span class="lrh-desc">The direction vector can steer outputs (steering vector)</span>
    </div>
    <p class="unify-note">Under the causal inner product, all three unify to the same direction (Riesz isomorphism) — one vector serves both as linear probe and as steering vector</p>
  </div>
  <div class="evidence">
    <KV label="Classic evidence">Othello-GPT: relative-color coordinates (Mine/Yours/Empty) linear probe 75% → 99%</KV>
    <KV label="Continuous quantities (Gurnee & Tegmark)">Latitude/longitude, historical timestamps: linear probe vs nonlinear MLP gap R² &lt; 0.02</KV>
  </div>
  <div class="superposition-note">
    <span class="sp-label">Superposition</span>
    <span class="sp-text">n neurons represent &gt; n features (near-orthogonal directions). Individual neurons are polysemantic; features live on linear combinations — SAEs recover them from superposition</span>
  </div>
</Band>
