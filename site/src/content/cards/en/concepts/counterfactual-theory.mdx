  <h2 class="card-title">Counterfactual Theory</h2>
  <p class="card-subtitle">Counterfactual Theory of Causation — Lewis 1973</p>
</Band>

<Band tone="tan">
  <CoreDef>c causes e iff "had c not occurred, e would not have occurred." Causation is reduced to a relation between possible worlds — in the nearest world where c doesn't occur, e doesn't occur either.</CoreDef>
</Band>

<Band tone="paper-raised" grow>
  <div class="lewis-chain">
    <div class="lc-step">
      <span class="lc-n">1</span>
      <span class="lc-text">Counterfactual conditionals have truth conditions — analyzed via "nearest possible worlds" semantics</span>
    </div>
    <div class="lc-step">
      <span class="lc-n">2</span>
      <span class="lc-text">Causal dependence = counterfactual dependence: had c not occurred, e would not have</span>
    </div>
    <div class="lc-step">
      <span class="lc-n">3</span>
      <span class="lc-text">Causation = transitive closure of chains of causal dependence (Lewis later revised to an "influence" theory)</span>
    </div>
  </div>
  <div class="challenges">
    <Kicker>Main challenges</Kicker>
    <KV label="Preemption">Billy hits the target before Suzy — Billy caused it, but even without Billy, Suzy would have thrown; counterfactual dependence fails</KV>
    <KV label="Symmetric overdetermination">Two stones shatter the bottle simultaneously — which is "the" cause? Is either necessary?</KV>
    <KV label="Prevention">Bond shoots down the missile, preventing Blofeld's death — prevention is "absence-causation," physically no process</KV>
  </div>
</Band>

<Band tone="paper-sunk" dense>
  <p class="modern-note">Modern causal modeling (Pearl, Hitchcock) offers a graphical implementation of counterfactual semantics — intervene on a causal model, more operational than possible-worlds semantics.</p>
</Band>
