<Band tone="paper-raised" dense>
  <h2 class="card-title">Preemption</h2>
  <p class="card-subtitle">Preemption — a backup cause is present but the actual cause still preempts the effect</p>
</Band>

<Band tone="tan">
  <CoreDef>Preemption is direct proof that a cause need not be a necessary condition for its effect (the effect would still occur without c). This challenges counterfactual theories. Three varieties: early (backup chain cut before it completes), late (preempted after starting), trumping (backup process completes but is overridden).</CoreDef>
</Band>

<Band tone="paper-raised" grow>
  <div class="three-types">
    <div class="pt-row early">
      <div class="pt-header"><span class="pt-name">Early preemption</span><span class="pt-theory ok">counterfactual handles it</span></div>
      <span class="pt-desc">The backup chain is cut off before the effect. Suzy stays home (Billy says he'll do it), Billy smashes the window. Counterfactual theory handles it: if d doesn't happen, e doesn't happen.</span>
    </div>
    <div class="pt-row late">
      <div class="pt-header"><span class="pt-name">Late preemption</span><span class="pt-theory hard">counterfactual struggles</span></div>
      <span class="pt-desc">Billy and Suzy throw rocks simultaneously; Suzy's rock hits first and shatters the bottle, Billy's rock flies through the empty space. Lewis 1973 fails: the bottle shattering doesn't counterfactually depend on Suzy's throw. Structural-equation framework: freeze BH=0 to recover it.</span>
    </div>
    <div class="pt-row trump">
      <div class="pt-header"><span class="pt-name">Trumping preemption</span><span class="pt-theory hard">extremely hard for Lewis</span></div>
      <span class="pt-desc">Merlin casts early + Morgana casts late; the magic rule says the earlier spell wins. Morgana's spell completes but is trumped. Process theories handle this naturally; counterfactual theory needs the "influence" variant (Lewis 2000).</span>
    </div>
  </div>
  <KV label="Process-theory strength">there is a complete causal process from c to e while the backup b's process is cut off — preemption is process theory's home turf</KV>
</Band>
