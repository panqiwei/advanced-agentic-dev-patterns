<Band tone="paper-raised" dense>
  <h2 class="card-title">Causal Contextualism</h2>
  <p class="card-subtitle">Causal Contextualism — is the causal relation objective or context-dependent?</p>
</Band>

<Band tone="tan">
  <CoreDef>The core question: are causal judgments invariant like mass, or context-sensitive like "tall"? "Suzy stealing the coconut cake caused her sickness" — change the contrast and the truth value changes.</CoreDef>
</Band>

<Band tone="paper-raised" grow>
  <PillarGrid cols={3}>
    <Pillar label="Invariantism · Lewis">Causation is an objective relation; contexts only choose what to mention (pragmatics), not the causal truth</Pillar>
    <Pillar label="Contrastivism · Schaffer">Causation is 4-place: c rather than c* caused e rather than e*; context fixes the implicit contrasts</Pillar>
    <Pillar label="Contextualism">Same statement takes different truth values in different contexts — what "counts as" a cause itself shifts</Pillar>
  </PillarGrid>
</Band>

<Band tone="paper-sunk" dense>
  <div class="test-case">
    <p class="tc-title">Gardener & Queen test case</p>
    <p class="tc-body">Neither waters the plant, the plant dies; the counterfactual dependence is the same for both. Yet most people judge only the gardener's omission is the cause — because the Queen never had the habit and no one expected her to.</p>
    <div class="tc-responses">
      <span class="tc-r">Invariantism: both are causes, but the Queen's is not worth mentioning</span>
      <span class="tc-r">Contextualism: normality distinctions mean the Queen's omission does not "count" as a cause</span>
    </div>
  </div>
</Band>
