<Band tone="paper-raised" dense>
  <h2 class="card-title">Causal Intervention</h2>
  <p class="card-subtitle">Causal Intervention — the do-operator and "mini-surgery"</p>
</Band>

<Band tone="tan">
  <CoreDef>Intervention = cut a variable's original causal inputs and clamp it to a specified value. Pearl calls it "mini-surgery" on the DAG: delete every arrow pointing into the intervened variable. That makes do(X=x) and P(Y|X=x) fundamentally different.</CoreDef>
</Band>

<Band tone="paper-raised" grow>
  <div class="contrast-block">
    <div class="contrast-row">
      <span class="c-label">OBSERVE</span>
      <span class="c-formula">P(recover | take drug)</span>
      <span class="c-note">May mix selection bias — healthier people are more likely to take the drug</span>
    </div>
    <div class="contrast-divider">⚡</div>
    <div class="contrast-row highlight">
      <span class="c-label c-do">INTERVENE</span>
      <span class="c-formula c-do">P(recover | do(take drug))</span>
      <span class="c-note c-do">Randomization severs selection bias; do deletes "why the drug was taken"</span>
    </div>
  </div>
  <div class="two-paths">
    <Kicker>Two paths to realize it</Kicker>
    <div class="path-row">
      <span class="path-label">RCT (experimental)</span>
      <span class="path-desc">Randomization makes treatment assignment independent of every confounder — direct, but not always feasible</span>
    </div>
    <div class="path-row">
      <span class="path-label">do-calculus (formal)</span>
      <span class="path-desc">Backdoor/frontdoor adjustment convert a do-expression into estimable observational probabilities</span>
    </div>
  </div>
</Band>

<Band tone="paper-sunk" dense>
  <Formula>P(Y | do(X=x)) = Σ<sub>z</sub> P(Y | X=x, Z=z) P(Z=z)</Formula>
  <p class="pearl-note">Pearl 2010: the core of causation is signal transmission and detection, not an experimenter's physical manipulation — "no manipulation, no causation" should be rejected.</p>
</Band>
