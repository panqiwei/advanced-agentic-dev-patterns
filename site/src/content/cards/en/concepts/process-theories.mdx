  <p class="card-subtitle">Process Theories — causation = physical-process connection: transfer of energy / momentum / conserved quantity between world-lines</p>
</Band>

<Band tone="tan">
  <CoreDef>Salmon (1984): causal processes can transmit marks, pseudo-processes cannot — a moving ball vs. a moving shadow. Dowe (2000) conserved-quantity theory: a causal process is a world-line possessing a conserved quantity (energy, momentum, charge); a causal interaction is an exchange of conserved quantities at the intersection of world-lines.</CoreDef>
</Band>
