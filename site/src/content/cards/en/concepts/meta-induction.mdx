<Band tone="paper-raised" dense>
  <h2 class="card-title">Meta-Induction</h2>
  <p class="card-subtitle">Meta-Induction — don't predict the world directly; track which inductive strategy is most accurate</p>
</Band>

<Band tone="tan">
  <CoreDef>Object-level induction predicts events; meta-induction tracks and combines the past performance of many object-level inducers. Weighted Meta-Induction (wMI) dynamically assigns weights by historical accuracy — even without answering "why induction works," it guarantees low regret against the best available strategy.</CoreDef>
</Band>

<Band tone="paper-raised" grow>
  <div class="two-levels">
    <Kicker>Two-level structure</Kicker>
    <div class="level-row">
      <div class="level-card obj">
        <span class="lv-label">Object-level induction</span>
        <span class="lv-desc">Predict directly from data: Bayes, neural nets, domain models…</span>
        <span class="lv-note">All have trade-offs; no universal best (NFL)</span>
      </div>
      <div class="level-card meta">
        <span class="lv-label">Meta-level</span>
        <span class="lv-desc">Track each object-strategy's performance → dynamic weighted combination</span>
        <span class="lv-note">wMI: weight ∝ historical accuracy</span>
      </div>
    </div>
  </div>
  <div class="wmi-props">
    <Kicker>wMI's three guarantees</Kicker>
    <div class="prop-row"><span class="p-n">①</span><span class="p-text">Bounded long-run regret: never persistently lags the best known strategy</span></div>
    <div class="prop-row"><span class="p-n">②</span><span class="p-text">No need to assume which object strategy is "correct" — weights reshuffle as the environment shifts</span></div>
    <div class="prop-row"><span class="p-n">③</span><span class="p-text">Bandit equivalence: each object strategy is an arm; wMI = adaptive multi-armed bandit</span></div>
  </div>
  <KV label="Philosophical upshot">Doesn't prove induction's absolute reliability; proves meta-induction's superiority over the best available strategy — an engineering-grade partial answer to Hume</KV>
</Band>
