<Band tone="tan">
  <CoreDef>Karpathy (2023)'s core claim: LLMs only have System 1 — each token costs roughly the same compute (one forward pass), regardless of problem difficulty. "Trains on rails, chunk chunk chunk." LLMs cannot "stop and think hard" — this is the architectural motivation behind CoT, Tree-of-Thoughts, and the o1/o3 line.</CoreDef>
</Band>

<Band tone="paper-raised" grow>
  <div class="contrast">

    <div class="ct-grid">
      <div class="ct-cell ct-s1">
        <span class="ct-name">System 1</span>
        <span class="ct-chars">fast · intuitive · automatic</span>
        <span class="ct-ex">"2+2=?" → answer instantly available</span>
        <span class="ct-llm">Today's LLM: fixed compute per token</span>
      </div>
      <div class="ct-cell ct-s2">
        <span class="ct-name">System 2</span>
        <span class="ct-chars">slow · rational · conscious</span>
        <span class="ct-ex">"17×24=?" → step-by-step derivation</span>
        <span class="ct-llm">Goal: allocate compute on demand</span>
      </div>
    </div>
  </div>
  <div class="engineering-s2">
    <Kicker>Paths to engineered System 2</Kicker>
    <KV label="CoT prompting">"Show your reasoning" → makes room for reasoning inside the token stream</KV>
    <KV label="o1/o3 family">Explicitly spend more inference time on hard problems — Karpathy 2023's prediction fulfilled</KV>
    <KV label="Kahneman's caveat">S1/S2 are not independent neural substrates but cognitive shortcuts — literally engineering two systems may misread the frame</KV>
    <KV label="Kautz Type-6 mapping">The end-goal of neurosymbolic AI — symbolic reasoning triggered inside the neural engine = S1 integrating S2</KV>
  </div>
