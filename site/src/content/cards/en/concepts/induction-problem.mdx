<Band tone="tan">
  <CoreDef>From "aspirin has always relieved headaches" to "it will this time too" — the inference feels natural, yet every rational path fails. Deductive reasoning cannot close past and future; probabilistic reasoning presupposes the very uniformity principle it tries to prove (vicious circle).</CoreDef>
</Band>

<Band tone="paper-raised" grow>
  <div class="hume-fork">
    <Kicker>Hume's Fork: only two kinds of argument</Kicker>
    <div class="fork-row">
      <div class="fork-col demo">
        <span class="fc-head">Demonstrative (deductive)</span>
        <span class="fc-result fail">✗ Fails</span>
        <p>The negation of UP is not a contradiction — a change in nature's course is logically conceivable</p>
      </div>
      <div class="fork-col prob">
        <span class="fc-head">Probable (inductive)</span>
        <span class="fc-result fail">✗ Circular</span>
        <p>A probabilistic argument itself invokes UP ("past probabilities extrapolate") — it already presupposes UP</p>
      </div>
    </div>
  </div>
  <div class="responses">
    <Kicker>Main responses</Kicker>
    <KV label="Popper (falsificationism)">Accept it as unsolvable; argue that science does not need induction at all</KV>
    <KV label="Bayesian approach">Reformulate with probability updating; offers the most precise mathematical treatment</KV>
    <KV label="Meta-induction (Schurz)">Don't defend induction directly; prove that "follow the most successful method so far" is a priori optimal as a meta-strategy</KV>
    <KV label="Hume himself">Custom and habit — the actual basis of induction, needing no rational justification</KV>
  </div>
</Band>
