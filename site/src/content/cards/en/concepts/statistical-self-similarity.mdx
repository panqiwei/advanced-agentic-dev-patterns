  <h2 class="card-title">Statistical Self-Similarity</h2>
  <p class="card-subtitle">Statistical Self-Similarity — natural objects keep the same statistical properties under different observation scales</p>
</Band>

<Band tone="tan">
  <CoreDef>Statistical self-similarity: an object's statistical properties (variance, distribution shape, power-law exponent) stay constant across scales — not every detail identical (that's strict self-similarity). Coastlines, clouds, vascular trees, earthquake sequences all qualify. This is the theoretical basis of Richardson's coastline paradox: as measurement scale shrinks, length grows without bound because every scale reveals new similar structure.</CoreDef>
</Band>

    <Kicker>Bridges</Kicker>
    <KV label="Richardson effect">As scale ε shrinks, coastline length L(ε) grows by a power law; the exponent is the fractal dimension D</KV>
    <KV label="Mandelbrot formalization">Statistical self-similarity → motivates fractal dimension → rigorous definition of "non-integer dimension"</KV>
    <KV label="Ubiquity in nature">Turbulence energy spectra, financial price fluctuations, word-frequency distributions — behind most power laws lies statistical self-similarity</KV>
    <KV label="Engineering implication">Sensor resolution improvements do not stop revealing new information — the geometric basis for diminishing returns on precision</KV>
