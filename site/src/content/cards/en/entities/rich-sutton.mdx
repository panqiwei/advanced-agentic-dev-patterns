  <p class="card-subtitle">Canadian computer scientist, reinforcement learning pioneer — "The Bitter Lesson" as the theoretical backbone of agent engineering</p>
</Band>

<Band tone="tan">
  <CoreDef>Sutton's 2019 essay The Bitter Lesson argues that 70 years of AI research show general computational methods (search + learning) always win in the end — baked-in human knowledge is effective in the short term but blocks long-term progress. This claim became the theoretical basis for "keep stripping assumptions" and meta-harness architectures in agent harness engineering.</CoreDef>
</Band>

  <div class="implications">
    <Kicker>Implications for Agent Engineering</Kicker>
    <KV label="Keep Stripping Assumptions">Today's effective harness constraints will be replaced by future model capabilities — design with a stripping path in mind</KV>
    <KV label="Meta-Harness">Don't encode specific strategies; encode mechanisms that let the model discover strategies — the Bitter Lesson in harness practice</KV>
    <KV label="Theory vs. Engineering">Sutton doesn't do agent engineering, but his framework is the key lens for understanding how harnesses evolve</KV>
