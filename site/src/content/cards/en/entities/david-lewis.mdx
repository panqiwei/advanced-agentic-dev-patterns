<Band tone="paper-raised" dense>
  <h2 class="card-title">David Lewis</h2>
  <p class="card-subtitle">American–Australian analytic philosopher (1941–2001) — founder of the counterfactual theory of causation</p>
</Band>

<Band tone="tan">
  <CoreDef>Lewis (1973): c is a cause of e iff there is a chain of counterfactual dependence from c to e — "if c had not occurred, e would not have occurred" — with precise truth conditions supplied by closest-possible-world semantics. Lewis also held that type causation is just a generalization of token causation, and that causation is an objective, context-independent relation (egalitarian invariantism).</CoreDef>
</Band>

<Band tone="paper-raised" grow>
  <div class="theories">
    <Kicker>Evolution of the theory of causation</Kicker>
    <div class="th-row th-1973">
      <span class="th-year">1973</span>
      <span class="th-name">Counterfactual theory</span>
      <span class="th-desc">Dependence-chain model + possible-world semantics — a paradigm shift in the metaphysics of causation</span>
    </div>
    <div class="th-row th-1979">
      <span class="th-year">1979</span>
      <span class="th-name">Temporal asymmetry</span>
      <span class="th-desc">"Asymmetry of overdetermination" explains causation's temporal direction — causes leave abundant traces that are overdetermined by their effects</span>
    </div>
    <div class="th-row th-1986">
      <span class="th-year">1986</span>
      <span class="th-name">Fine-grained events</span>
      <span class="th-desc">Events are properties of spatiotemporal regions — resolves cases like distinguishing the rotation vs. the heating of a metal ball</span>
    </div>
    <div class="th-row th-2000">
      <span class="th-year">2000</span>
      <span class="th-name">Influence theory</span>
      <span class="th-desc">Response to late/trumping preemption: shift from "counterfactual dependence" to "influence" — but faces spurious-causation critiques</span>
    </div>
  </div>
  <div class="positions">
    <Kicker>Core positions</Kicker>
    <KV label="Modal realism">Non-actual possible worlds are concrete entities as real as the actual world (a stance most philosophers keep at arm's length)</KV>
    <KV label="Contrast with Pearl">Lewis's possible-world semantics vs. Pearl's interventionist semantics (do-calculus) — two formalizations of causation set in contrast</KV>
  </div>
</Band>
