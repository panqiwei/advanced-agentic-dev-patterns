  <CoreDef>Chroma's 2025 Context Rot study is one of the most systematic empirical works on LLM long-context degradation to date: 18 frontier models tested across 4 controlled experiment classes (needle-question similarity, distractor impact, needle-haystack similarity, haystack structure), plus LongMemEval and Repeated Words as supplementary experiments. Code open-sourced at github.com/chroma-core/context-rot.</CoreDef>
</Band>

<Band tone="paper-raised" grow>
  <div class="research">
    <Kicker>Context Rot — study design</Kicker>
    <KV label="Scale">18 frontier models, 4 controlled experiment classes + 2 supplementary — the most systematic long-context degradation study to date</KV>
    <KV label="Needle vs haystack similarity">Needle similar to the question → easier to find; needle similar to haystack → easier to confuse</KV>
    <KV label="Distractor impact">Relevant-but-incorrect distractors cause large performance drops — structured context outperforms random ordering</KV>
    <KV label="LongMemEval">Cross-session memory evaluation — systematic degradation of information retrieval across multi-turn dialogue</KV>
  </div>
