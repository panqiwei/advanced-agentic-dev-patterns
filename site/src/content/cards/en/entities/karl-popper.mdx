<Band tone="paper-raised" dense>
  <h2 class="card-title">Karl Popper</h2>
  <p class="card-subtitle">Austrian/British philosopher of science (1902–1994) — founder of falsificationism and leading critical rationalist</p>
</Band>

<Band tone="tan">
  <CoreDef>Popper accepts Hume's problem of induction as unsolvable, but argues science does not rely on induction. The falsificationist program: science advances through bold conjectures and stringent falsification, not by accumulating inductive confirmations. The logic of falsification is purely deductive (modus tollens): H → P, ¬P, therefore ¬H. A theory that has not been falsified is not "confirmed" — only "corroborated."</CoreDef>
</Band>

<Band tone="paper-raised" grow>
  <div class="method">
    <Kicker>The falsificationist method</Kicker>
    <div class="pm-row pm-demarcation">
      <span class="pm-name">Falsifiability as criterion</span>
      <span class="pm-desc">The hallmark of science: producing predictions that could be empirically refuted — demarcating science from non-science</span>
    </div>
    <div class="pm-row pm-corroboration">
      <span class="pm-name">Corroboration vs. confirmation</span>
      <span class="pm-desc">A theory not yet falsified is not inductively "confirmed," only provisionally "corroborated" — refusing the logic of confirmation</span>
    </div>
    <div class="pm-row pm-asymmetry">
      <span class="pm-name">Asymmetry of confirmation and falsification</span>
      <span class="pm-desc">No finite set of positive instances can confirm a universal claim, yet a single counterexample can falsify it — exploiting this logical asymmetry</span>
    </div>
  </div>
  <div class="criticism">
    <Kicker>Core limitations</Kicker>
    <KV label="Induction smuggled back in">The notion of "corroboration": when several non-falsified hypotheses conflict, why prefer the better-corroborated one? Induction re-enters quietly</KV>
    <KV label="Contrast with Hume">Same starting point — accepting the induction problem — but different exits: Popper turns to deduction, Hume to custom</KV>
    <KV label="ML analogy">Falsifiability ↔ testable model predictions; corroboration ↔ validation-set performance — a structural mapping between philosophy of science and ML</KV>
  </div>
</Band>
