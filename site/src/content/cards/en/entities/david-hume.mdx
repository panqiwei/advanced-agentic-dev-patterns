  <h2 class="card-title">David Hume</h2>
  <p class="card-subtitle">Scottish philosopher (1711–1776) — founder of empiricist epistemology and a turning point in Western philosophy</p>
</Band>

<Band tone="tan">
  <CoreDef>Modeling himself on Newton, Hume imported the experimental method into the "moral sciences." Core chain of influence: Kant was awakened by Hume from his "dogmatic slumber," and the entire critical philosophy is a response to Hume's challenge; Darwin regarded Hume as a central influence on evolutionary thought; reading Hume made "the scales fall" from Bentham's eyes, seeding the utilitarian tradition. One of the most important philosophers in the English-speaking world.</CoreDef>
</Band>

<Band tone="paper-raised" grow>
  <div class="core-concepts">
    <Kicker>Core contributions to the theory of causation</Kicker>
    <div class="cc-row cc-const">
      <span class="cc-name">Constant conjunction</span>
      <span class="cc-desc">The empirical basis of causation — a "cause" is a constant conjunction "regularly followed by" its effect, not a mysterious necessary connection</span>
    </div>
    <div class="cc-row cc-nec">
      <span class="cc-name">Problem of necessary connection</span>
      <span class="cc-desc">Necessary connection is not given in impressions; it arises from the mind's expectation shaped by constant conjunction — exposing the subjective source of causation</span>
    </div>
    <div class="cc-row cc-ind">
      <span class="cc-name">Problem of induction</span>
      <span class="cc-desc">The uniformity principle cannot be demonstrated (demonstrative reasoning) nor justified (probabilistic reasoning is circular) — reason is powerless before induction</span>
    </div>
    <div class="cc-row cc-habit">
      <span class="cc-name">The role of custom</span>
      <span class="cc-desc">A more reliable practical guide than reason — custom, not reason, drives human predictive behavior</span>
    </div>
  </div>
