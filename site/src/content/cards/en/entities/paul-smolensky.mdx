<Band tone="paper-raised" dense>
  <h2 class="card-title">Paul Smolensky</h2>
  <p class="card-subtitle">Cognitive scientist at Johns Hopkins — author of the most complete theoretical framework for connectionist cognitive science</p>
</Band>

<Band tone="tan">
  <CoreDef>Smolensky (1988) replied directly to the Fodor &amp; Pylyshyn symbolist critique by proposing the subconceptual level: connectionism operates between the neurobiological level and the symbolic/conceptual level, with symbolic rules as an approximate macroscopic description of subconceptual dynamics — not a literal description of underlying mechanisms. This is one of the most important target articles in the history of cognitive science (with 40+ peer commentaries).</CoreDef>
</Band>

<Band tone="paper-raised" grow>
  <div class="theories">
    <Kicker>Four Theoretical Contributions</Kicker>
    <div class="th-row th-harmony">
      <span class="tr-name">Harmony Theory (1986–1988)</span>
      <span class="tr-desc">Cognition as maximizing a global harmony function — measures how well activation states satisfy soft constraints encoded in the network weights</span>
    </div>
    <div class="th-row th-sub">
      <span class="tr-name">Subconceptual Level (1988)</span>
      <span class="tr-desc">The intermediate level of connectionism — symbolic rules are macroscopic approximations, not literal descriptions of underlying mechanisms</span>
    </div>
    <div class="th-row th-tensor">
      <span class="tr-name">Tensor Product Representation (1990)</span>
      <span class="tr-desc">Role-filler binding via tensor product superposition — the most systematic attempt to embed compositionality into connectionism</span>
    </div>
    <div class="th-row th-ot">
      <span class="tr-name">Optimality Theory OT (1993, with Prince)</span>
      <span class="tr-desc">Harmony theory applied to phonology — became the mainstream framework of modern generative phonology</span>
    </div>
  </div>
  <div class="legacy">
    <Kicker>Historical Standing and Impact</Kicker>
    <KV label="The Fodor Debate">Two 1988 papers — cognitive science's most famous theoretical debate, still not fully resolved</KV>
    <KV label="Vindicated by Modern LLMs">LLM emergent abilities and implicit rules are exactly "subconceptual dynamics producing macroscopic symbolic behavior" made empirical</KV>
    <KV label="Neurosymbolic AI">Garcez &amp; Lamb's third wave of neurosymbolic AI partly inherits from the subconceptual-level theory</KV>
  </div>
</Band>
