<Band tone="paper-raised" dense>
  <h2 class="card-title">Thomas Bayes</h2>
  <p class="card-subtitle">English minister and mathematician (1701–1761) — originator of the inverse-probability method, namesake of Bayes' theorem</p>
</Band>

<Band tone="tan">
  <CoreDef>Bayes pioneered the "inverse probability" method — inferring the probability of a hypothesis from an observed sample. His paper (1764, posthumous, edited by Richard Price) may be the direct reply to Hume's 1748 Enquiry Concerning Human Understanding — using probability theory to give "reasoning from past to future" a mathematical foundation.</CoreDef>
</Band>

<Band tone="paper-raised" grow>
  <div class="core">
    <Kicker>The Logic of Inverse Probability</Kicker>
    <div class="ip-row ip-direct">
      <span class="ip-label">Direct Probability (Classical)</span>
      <span class="ip-desc">Given hypothesis H, compute the probability of observation E: P(E | H)</span>
    </div>
    <div class="ip-row ip-inverse">
      <span class="ip-label">Inverse Probability (Bayes' Contribution)</span>
      <span class="ip-desc">Given observation E, compute the probability that H is true: P(H | E) ∝ P(E | H) · P(H)</span>
    </div>
    <div class="ip-row ip-hume">
      <span class="ip-label">Relation to Hume</span>
      <span class="ip-desc">Hume asks: why do past regularities apply to the future? Bayes: every new observation updates hypothesis confidence — induction is probability updating</span>
    </div>
  </div>
  <div class="legacy">
    <Kicker>Historical Standing</Kicker>
    <KV label="Posthumous Publication">Edited by Richard Price after his 1761 death, published 1764 in Philosophical Transactions</KV>
    <KV label="Laplace's Independent Development">Laplace later generalized and gave Bayes' theorem its full mathematical form</KV>
    <KV label="Precise Reply to Induction">The Bayesian framework recasts induction's legitimacy from "true/false" to "confidence updating" — one of the most precise replies to the induction problem</KV>
    <KV label="Applications in AI">Bayesian inference is core to machine learning — from naive Bayes to MCMC, "inverse probability" runs through the whole of AI</KV>
  </div>
</Band>
