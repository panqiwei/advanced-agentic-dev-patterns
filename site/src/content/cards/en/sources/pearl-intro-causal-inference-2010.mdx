  <h2 class="card-title">An Introduction to Causal Inference · Pearl 2010</h2>
  <p class="card-subtitle">The paradigm shift from association to causation — the unified framework of the Structural Causal Model (SCM)</p>
</Band>

<Band tone="tan">
  <CoreDef>A <span class="em">crisp and clear dividing line</span> — associational concepts are definable from the observed distribution (correlation, regression, conditional independence); <span class="em">causal concepts are not</span> (randomization, effect, intervention, attribution). Every causal claim rests on at least one causal premise.</CoreDef>
</Band>

<Band tone="paper-raised" grow>
  <div class="method">
    <div class="mt-head">Four-step methodology · Define–Assume–Identify–Estimate</div>
    <div class="steps">
      <div class="st st--1">
        <div class="st-n">01</div>
        <div class="st-k">Define</div>
        <div class="st-v">Define the target causal quantity Q(M) as an algorithm computable on any fully specified model — independent of parametric form</div>
      </div>
      <div class="st st--2">
        <div class="st-n">02</div>
        <div class="st-k">Assume</div>
        <div class="st-v">Encode causal assumptions with graphs and structural equations — <span class="em">missing arrows matter more than present ones</span></div>
      </div>
      <div class="st st--3">
        <div class="st-n">03</div>
        <div class="st-k">Identify</div>
        <div class="st-v">Decide whether the target quantity is estimable from observational data — reduce <span class="mono">do</span>-expressions to do-free expressions</div>
      </div>
      <div class="st st--4">
        <div class="st-n">04</div>
        <div class="st-k">Estimate</div>
        <div class="st-v">Estimate the identified quantity with statistical methods; test the model's testable implications</div>
      </div>
    </div>
  </div>

  <div class="tools">
    <Kicker>Core tools</Kicker>
    <div class="tl"><span class="tl-k">do-operator</span><span class="tl-v">"Model surgery" — delete equations pointing to the intervened variable, replace with a constant</span></div>
    <div class="tl"><span class="tl-k">Back-door</span><span class="tl-v">Sufficient adjustment set — no descendants of X, blocks all back-door paths</span></div>
    <div class="tl"><span class="tl-k">Front-door</span><span class="tl-v">When confounders are unobservable, estimate in two steps via an observable mediator</span></div>
    <div class="tl"><span class="tl-k">Counterfactual 3-step</span><span class="tl-v">Abduction → Action → Prediction (modify M_x, solve)</span></div>
  </div>
</Band>
