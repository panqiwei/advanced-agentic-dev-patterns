  <h2 class="card-title">The Hidden Cost of Structure · Trajectory Bias</h2>
  <p class="card-subtitle">Even when syntax is perfectly legal — structural constraints <span class="em">systematically hurt</span> semantic correctness</p>
</Band>

<Band tone="tan" dense>
  <CoreDef>
    <span class="em">Trajectory Bias</span> — by masking and renormalizing tokens at every step, constrained decoding pushes the model's <span class="em">reasoning trajectory</span> toward "prefixes that are easier to keep structurally legal," even when those prefixes correspond to wrong answers.
  </CoreDef>
</Band>

<Band tone="paper-raised" grow>
  <div class="mech">
    <div class="mh-head">The two-step perturbation of constrained decoding</div>
    <div class="mh-step">
      <div class="ms-n">1</div>
      <div class="ms-k">Mask illegal tokens</div>
      <div class="ms-v">Zero out the probability of any token violating the grammar constraint</div>
    </div>
    <div class="mh-step">
      <div class="ms-n">2</div>
      <div class="ms-k">Renormalize</div>
      <div class="ms-v">Redistribute probability over the remaining legal tokens — when strict formatting forces low-entropy syntactic decisions, renormalization becomes a <span class="em">large perturbation</span></div>
    </div>

    <div class="prob-vis">
      <div class="pv-label">Natural-language tendency</div>
      <div class="pv-bar pv-bar--natural">
        <div class="pv-seg pv-seg--p" style="width:42%">P(good)</div>
        <div class="pv-seg pv-seg--s" style="width:12%">P(quote)</div>
        <div class="pv-seg pv-seg--s" style="width:10%">P(brace)</div>
        <div class="pv-seg pv-seg--o" style="width:36%">other</div>
      </div>
      <div class="pv-arrow">↓ constrained decoding</div>
      <div class="pv-label">Post-constraint distribution (low confidence)</div>
      <div class="pv-bar pv-bar--constrained">
        <div class="pv-seg pv-seg--forced" style="width:55%">{'P(")'}</div>
        <div class="pv-seg pv-seg--forced2" style="width:45%">{'P({)'}</div>
      </div>
    </div>
  </div>
</Band>

<Band tone="paper-sunk" dense>
  <div class="findings">
    <div class="fd"><span class="fk">Base vs SFT</span><span class="fv">Base models adapt to structural constraints better — conversational SFT <span class="em">conflicts</span> with structural constraints, <span class="warn">current SFT may inadvertently weaken structured-output ability</span></span></div>
    <div class="fd"><span class="fk">Few-shot asymmetric</span><span class="fv">Constrained models gain from few-shot at a steeper slope — once natural-language flexibility is stripped, few-shot becomes the main compensation</span></div>
    <div class="fd"><span class="fk">Train-time integration</span><span class="fv">The current "train fluent dialogue + apply constraints at inference" paradigm is fundamentally misaligned — integration should happen during training</span></div>
  </div>
</Band>
