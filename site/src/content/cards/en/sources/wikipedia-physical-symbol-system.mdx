<Band tone="paper-raised" dense>
  <h2 class="card-title">The Physical Symbol System Hypothesis (PSSH)</h2>
  <p class="card-subtitle">Manipulate physical symbols, compose structures, process them to generate new expressions — necessary and sufficient for general intelligence?</p>
</Band>

<Band tone="tan">
  <CoreDef>
    <span class="em">The PSSH claim</span>: a physical symbol system has the necessary and sufficient means for general intelligent action — human cognition operates by symbol manipulation, and machines can achieve intelligence by similar processes.
  </CoreDef>
</Band>

<Band tone="paper-raised" grow>
  <div class="debate">
    <div class="side side--pro">
      <div class="sd-head">
        <span class="sd-tag sd-tag--pro">✓ Support</span>
        <span class="sd-title">Empirical evidence</span>
      </div>
      <ul class="sd-list">
        <li><strong>Psychology</strong> — Newell/Simon experiments: humans solve logic problems via step-by-step symbol manipulation</li>
        <li><strong>Early AI wins</strong> (1950-60s): STUDENT · Logic Theorist · Samuel's checkers · ELIZA · SHRDLU</li>
        <li><strong>Formal-logic lineage</strong>: Frege · Russell · Turing machine · stored-program computers · LISP (1959-60)</li>
      </ul>
    </div>
    <div class="side side--con">
      <div class="sd-head">
        <span class="sd-tag sd-tag--con">✗ Challenge</span>
        <span class="sd-title">Arguments against</span>
      </div>
      <ul class="sd-list">
        <li><strong>Dreyfus</strong> — experts rely on unconscious intuition, not explicit symbol manipulation</li>
        <li><strong>Embodied cognition</strong> (Lakoff) — abstract reasoning is rooted in embodied skill</li>
        <li><strong>Commonsense hard problems</strong> — frame / qualification / ramification problem</li>
        <li><strong>Moravec's paradox</strong> — sensorimotor skill ≫ abstract reasoning</li>
        <li><strong>Brooks</strong> — "the world is its own best model"</li>
        <li><strong>Deep learning</strong> (2012 AlexNet) — connectionist methods decisively outperformed symbolic systems</li>
      </ul>
    </div>
  </div>
</Band>

<Band tone="paper-sunk" dense>
  <div class="dist">
    <Kicker>Three essential distinctions</Kicker>
    <div class="dt"><span class="dt-k">Semantic symbols vs dynamic signals</span><span class="dt-v">PSSH concerns symbols with referential meaning, not raw bits or uninterpreted activations</span></div>
    <div class="dt"><span class="dt-k">General intelligence vs narrow capability</span><span class="dt-v">PSSH targets AGI — current AI has not realized this; the critique is prospective</span></div>
    <div class="dt"><span class="dt-k">Intelligent behavior vs consciousness</span><span class="dt-v">Concerns behavior only — not Searle's "strong AI" claim</span></div>
  </div>
