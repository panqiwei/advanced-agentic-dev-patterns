    <Kicker>Zhichang vs Li Bo</Kicker>
    <p class="st-body">Li Bo, Tang-dynasty prefect of Jiangzhou, doubted "a mustard seed holds Mt. Sumeru". Master Zhichang replied by pointing at Li Bo himself — <span class="em">a human head is no larger than a coconut, yet it holds ten thousand books</span>. Capacity is not bound by physical dimension; it depends on the nature of what is contained.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="story">
    <Kicker>One grain of rice calms the river</Kicker>
    <p class="st-body">A disciple of the Buddha tried to still a raging river with Mt. Sumeru itself and failed; yet when the Buddha lightly dropped <span class="em">a single grain of rice</span>, the river grew quiet — one grain of rice had accumulated immeasurable merit from seeding to ripening, and <span class="em">a single reverent thought</span> equates the small with Mt. Sumeru.</p>
