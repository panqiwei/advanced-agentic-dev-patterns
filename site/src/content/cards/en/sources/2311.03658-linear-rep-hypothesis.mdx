<Band tone="tan">
  <CoreDef>"Linear representation" is empirically famous — but what does it actually mean? This paper distinguishes three different intuitions (subspace / measurement / intervention), formalizes "concept" with counterfactual variables, and introduces the causal inner product — the unique inner product under which causally separable concepts are orthogonal, unifying all three intuitions in one mathematical frame.</CoreDef>
</Band>

    <Kicker>Key properties of the causal inner product</Kicker>
    <KV label="Definition">The unique inner product that makes "causally separable concepts orthogonal"</KV>
    <KV label="Form">⟨γ̄, γ̄'⟩_C = γ̄ᵀ Cov(γ)⁻¹ γ̄' (weighted by the inverse vocab-unembedding covariance)</KV>
    <KV label="LLaMA-2 7B validation">Block-diagonal structure over 27 concepts — semantically related concepts share blocks, causally separable ones are approximately orthogonal</KV>
