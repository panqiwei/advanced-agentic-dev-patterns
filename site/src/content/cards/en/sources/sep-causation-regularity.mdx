<Band tone="paper-sunk" dense>
  <div class="takeaway">
    <Kicker>Five key conclusions</Kicker>
    <ul class="tk-list">
      <li>Regularity theories <span class="em">are not obsolete</span> — Lewis 1973's classic objections have been answered</li>
      <li>Inferential theories are the <span class="em">natural heirs</span> of regularity theories — inferential relations replace law-instantiation</li>
      <li><span class="mono">INUS</span> conditions are the pivotal bridge between the regularity and inferential traditions</li>
      <li>Contemporary versions handle overdetermination, early/late preemption, and switching cases</li>
      <li>The Best-Systems view of laws (Ramsey-Lewis) grounds the distinction between laws and accidental regularities</li>
    </ul>
  </div>
